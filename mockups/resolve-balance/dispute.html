Dispute this information

You have the right to dispute any data SoFi has reported to Early Warning Services (EWS) that you believe is inaccurate. Disputes can be submitted in two ways. Select one below.

Direct dispute to SoFi

How to file

  1. Download the dispute form (PDF).
  2. Fill in all required fields.
  3. Mail the completed form to the address below. Keep a copy for your records.
  4. SoFi will notify Early Warning Services and suppress your record while we investigate.
  5. You will receive our written response within 5 days of review completion.

What to include

  • Your full name
  • Your Social Security Number
  • Account and routing number for the record being disputed
  • A written statement describing the data you are disputing (700 characters max)

Review timelines

30 calendar days Standard review deadline from date of receipt.
21 calendar days For Maine residents.
45 calendar days If you provide new information during the investigation, the review window extends to 45 calendar days.
5 calendar days SoFi will send you a written response within 5 calendar days of completing the review, including outcome details and your rights (including the right to submit a rebuttal statement if your dispute is not upheld).
5 business days If your dispute is determined to be frivolous or irrelevant (e.g. duplicate submission or insufficient information), SoFi will notify you within 5 business days with the reason and what additional information is needed.

Identity theft dispute

When to use this

Use this dispute type if you believe someone opened this account or incurred charges without your knowledge or consent during the disputed time period.

What to include

  • A statement alleging identity theft during the time frame of the disputed activity.
  • A statement that the record(s), transaction(s), or inquiry(s) are not yours.
  • A copy of an accepted identity theft report. Accepted forms include:
    • FTC Identity Theft affidavit (identitytheft.gov)
    • Police department report
    • Identity theft report filed with a state Attorney General
    • US Postal Inspection Service report

What happens after you file

Step 1 SoFi places an ID theft flag on the record and stops furnishing related information to EWS while the investigation is open.
Step 2 If EWS determines the dispute qualifies, they place a block on the applicable record in their system and submit an investigation request to SoFi.
If upheld SoFi agrees the dispute is valid. The EWS block remains in place and the record stays suppressed.
If denied SoFi determines the reported information is accurate, provides supporting documentation to EWS. EWS unblocks the record and notifies you that it was reinserted.
